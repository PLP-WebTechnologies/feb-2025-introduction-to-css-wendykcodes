5 Key Tips for Healthy Natural Hair

1. Moisturize Consistently

Natural hair tends to be dry due to its coiled structure, which makes it harder for natural oils to travel down the hair shaft.

  • Use water-based leave-ins or aloe vera juice to hydrate.
  • Seal moisture in with natural oils (like coconut, olive, or jojoba) or butters (like shea or mango).
  • Incorporate deep conditioning weekly or bi-weekly.

2. Gentle Handling and Protective Styling

Minimize breakage by treating your hair gently.

  • Detangle with your fingers or a wide-tooth comb when hair is damp and conditioned.
  • Use protective styles to reduce manipulation.
  • Avoid tight styles that put tension on your edges or scalp.

3. Use Sulfate-Free, Natural Hair Products

Many commercial shampoos are too harsh and strip natural oils.

  • Opt for sulfate-free shampoos or co-wash with cleansing conditioners.
  • Read labels and avoid drying alcohols and mineral oils that can clog hair follicles or dry out your hair.

4. Trim Regularly

Split ends can travel up the hair shaft and cause more damage.

  • Trim every 8-12 weeks, or as needed, to maintain healthy ends.
  • Don't wait for major damage. Preventive trim help retain length over time.

5. Protect Hair at Night

Night-time care is just as important as daily maintenance.

  • Sleep on a satin or silk pillowcase or use a satin bonnet/scarf.
  • This prevents moisture loss and reduces friction that can cause breakage.
